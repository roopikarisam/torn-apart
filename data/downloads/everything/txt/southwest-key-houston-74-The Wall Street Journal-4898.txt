July 10, 2018

 Southwest Airlines Co. said its third-quarter revenue rose despite two hurricanes, and sees “solid” fourth-quarter travel demand as ticket pricing has firmed in October from the prior two months. 

Southwest, the nation’s No. 4 carrier by traffic, said it expects its unit revenue—a key metric of how much money it earns for each seat flow a mile—to increase by up to 1.5% in the year’s final quarter, after slipping by 0.5% in the third quarter, the midpoint of its earlier guidance.
