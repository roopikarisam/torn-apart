A Portsmouth man was arrested Wednesday evening for alleged drug trafficking after authorities said they received previous information that the suspect was selling drugs in the area.


The Portsmouth Police Department targeted the area of 17th Street and Kendall Avenue.


Investigators made contact with the suspect and said they noticed a strong odor of marijuana. The suspect admitted to the marijuana, and was placed under arrest, but took off on foot.


A brief foot chase ensued until the suspect was apprehended. He was identified as 20-year-old Marquice Poore.


Police report they found nearly 20 grams of suspect methamphetamine, 5 grams of an unknown white powdery substance, suboxone strips, and more than $2,000 in cash. While processing the man into Scioto County Jail, it was found he had another plastic baggy of unknown substance inserted into his body cavity.


Poore has been charged with Trafficking in Drugs (F-2), Tampering with Evidence (F-3), Illegal Conveyance of Drugs into a Detention Facility (F-3), Obstructing Official Business (M-3), Criminal Trespassing (M-4).
