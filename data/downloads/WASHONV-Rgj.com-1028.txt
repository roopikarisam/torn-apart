A Reno man pleaded guilty charges of sexual exploitation of a minor and distribution of child pornography after authorities found photos of his infant daughter.



Derrick Joseph Rady, 36, admitted to using his cell phone to take exploitative photos of his infant daughter and distributing photos of a minor engaging in sexually explicit conduct, U.S. Attorney Dayle Elieson reported Monday.


According to the U.S. Attorney’s Office, sexual exploitation of a minor carries a mandatory minimum sentence of 15 years in prison and distribution of child pornography a mandatory minimum of five years in prison.

A statement from Elieson said Facebook and Google reported possible child pornography on their sites to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children. Subsequently, the Northern Nevada Child Exploitation Task Force executed search warrants at Rady’s home and and found multiple devices containing child pornography.
Sentencing is scheduled for June 27.
