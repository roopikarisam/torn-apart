    SAT Couple, set and no-partner dances from many countries, teaching 7:30 p.m., dances until 9:30 p.m. Saturday, Cedar Valley Grange, 20526 52nd Ave. W., Lynnwood; $6-$8 (949-646-7082 or sno-king.org).

    SUN Dance lesson, 8:45 a.m., dance to live Western Swing roots music, 1 p.m. Sunday, Lynnwood Eagles, 19223 Hwy 99, Lynnwood; free (nwwsms.com/events%20-%20copy.htm).

    Seattle Recycled Arts Festival

    FRI-SAT Art using reclaimed products, fashion show Friday only; outdoor sculptures, displays, art cars, fix-it clinic, donation drive, 6-9 p.m. Friday, noon-4 p.m. Saturday, Phinney Neighborhood Center, 6532 Phinney Ave. N, Seattle; free (206-783-2244 or facebook.com/SeattleRecycledArts/).

    Seattle Maritime Festival Family Fun Day

    SAT Tugboat industry celebration, working vessel simulator, boat building for kids, presentations on sea safety, oceanography; Survival Suit Races, Seattle Fire Boat water display and tours, food trucks, 11 a.m.-4 p.m. Saturday; entertainment stage with The Not Its!, noon; Knut Bell and the Blue Collars, 1:15 p.m.; The Knast, 2:30 p.m.; free Argosy Ship Canal Tours, space limited, tickets on-site only, 11:30 a.m., 1 and 2:30 p.m. Saturday; Seattle Maritime Academy, 4455 Shilshole Ave. N.W., Seattle (seattlemaritime101.com/harley-marine-maritime-festival).

    Spirit of Africa

    SAT African musicians, dancers and artists from our region and beyond, featuring African tama drum player Massamba Diop, featured in the “Black Panther” movie soundtrack; traditional and modern African clothing fashion show, African cuisine and cooking demonstrations, children’s art activities, African Marketplace, 11 a.m.-9 p.m. Saturday, Seattle Center Armory, Seattle (206-684-7200 or thespiritofafrica.org/).

    Strawberry Festival, Richmond Beach

    SAT Children’s Parade, ages 8 and younger invited to participate, “Superheroes” theme, 11:45 a.m., staging begins 11:15 a.m.; entertainment by local bands, food vendors including strawberry shortcake, noon-5 p.m. Saturday, Richmond Beach Community Park, 2201 N.W. 197th St., Shoreline; limited parking, off-site parking and shuttle service, Richmond Beach Congregational Church, corner of Richmond Beach Road and 15th Avenue Northwest (richmondbeachwa.org/events/strawberry/).

    SAT Celebrate wildlife, nature, and gardening with 50 art and craft vendors, educational exhibitors, hands on projects; Ella Bella Bee and the Pollinators performs at noon, guests include the Falconer and Ciscoe Morris, 10 a.m.-3 p.m., Saturday, Tukwila Community Center, 12424 42nd Ave. S., Tukwila (206-768-2822 or backyardwildlifefestival.org).

    Pike Place Market Flower Festival

    SAT-SUN Flowers, produce, and specialty products from more than 40 Washington state farmers in tents on the west side of the cobblestone street of Pike Place, 8 a.m.-5 p.m. Saturday-Sunday, Pike Place Market, First Avenue and Pike Street, Seattle (206-682-7453 or pikeplacemarket.org/market-events).

    Peony and Bamboo Festival

    SAT-SUN Highlights treasures of Chinese horticulture with plant sales, culture entertainment, displays, Chinese food vendors, activities for all ages, 10 a.m.-4 p.m. Saturday-Sunday, Seattle Chinese Garden, 6000 16th Ave. S.W., Seattle; suggested donation $5-$10 (206-934-5219 or seattlechinesegarden.org/).

    Syttende Mai

    MAY 17 Norway Constitution Day celebration; children’s activities, Fjord horses, 10 a.m.-5 p.m., musical performances and fashion show, 8 p.m., Nordic Heritage Museum, 2655 N.W. Market St.; musical entertainment, 2-6 p.m., Bergen Place Park, 22nd Avenue Northwest and Northwest Market Street; open house with heritage displays, entertainment, noon-5 p.m., Leif Erikson Hall; 17th of May Ballard Parade, 6 p.m. on 24th Avenue Northwest from Northwest 62nd Street to Northwest Market Street, Seattle, noon Thursday, Leif Erikson Hall, 2245 N.W. 57th St., Seattle; (17thofmay.org).

    A Glimpse of China Chinese Culture & Arts Festival

    PLAN AHEAD Creative traditions, performances, Chinese food vendors, sale of ethnic items, kids’ activities, painting and calligraphy demonstrations, Armory and Mural Amphitheater, May 19, Seattle Center, Seattle (206-684-7200 or chinaartandculture.org).

    PLAN AHEAD Includes all branches of the military, police and firefighters, youth organizations, car clubs; Heroes’ Barbeque free hot dog, chips and soda to all active-duty, reserve, retired and veteran service personnel, 10 a.m. May 19, Bremerton (bremertonchamber.org/pages/ArmedForcesDayParade).

    Paine Field Aviation Day

    PLAN AHEAD Flying Heritage & Combat Armor Museum and Historic Flight Foundation exhibits, food vendors, flight schools, flying demonstrations, 10 a.m.-5 p.m. May 19, Paine Field, 3407 109th St. S.W., Everett; $15, ages 9 and younger free (painefield.com/198/Paine-Field-Aviation-Day).

    U District Street Fair

    PLAN AHEAD Hundreds of craft and food vendors, music, beer garden, kids’ entertainment and activities, 10 a.m.-7 p.m. May 19, 10 a.m.-6 p.m. May 20, University Way, Seattle (udistrictstreetfair.org/).
