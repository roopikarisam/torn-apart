It's the last meal of the year. Make it a memorable one. 


Restaurants across the Valley are rolling out the red carpet for diners on New Year's Eve. All menus are offered on Sunday, Dec. 31. Note that most resorts have more than one dinner option.

The restaurant's inaugural New Year’s Eve Porch Party will boast unlimited food and drinks ($95) along with live entertainment and dancing from 8 p.m. to 1 a.m. Nibble on passed bites and stroll up to food stations showcasing dishes like fire-braised lamb tacos, grilled pheasant and foie gras mousse. Reservations recommended.


Details: 6000 E. Camelback Road, Phoenix. 480-423-2530, mowryandcotton.com.


Masa fried octopus with black bean puree and corn relish from Wright’s at The Biltmore at the 2017 azcentral Food &amp;amp; Wine Experience. (Photo: Dominic Armato/The Republic)

A luxurious five-course dinner ($195) features light bites like beet-cured scallop and tea-smoked ostrich. Other dishes include cast-iron duck breast with duck confit bread pudding; prime beef tenderloin with green-chile custard potato; and a white-chocolate raspberry pavé for dessert. A reception begins at 7 p.m., and the dinner will be served at 8. Reservations required.


Details: Arizona Biltmore, 2400 E. Missouri Ave., Phoenix. 602-955-6600, arizonabiltmore.com. 


The pan seared scallops at Citizen Public House. (Photo: Joanie Simon)

From 4 p.m. to midnight, The Gladly's three-course menu ($60) will feature options like kabocha squash, veal meatloaf, Oaxacan chile short rib, parsley-garlic chicken and black and tan pudding. At the same time, a different three-course menu ($60) will be served at its sister restaurant, Citizen Public House. Reservations recommended.


Details: The Gladly, 2201 E. Camelback Road, Phoenix. 602-759-8132, thegladly.com. Citizen Public House, 7111 E. Fifth Ave., Scottsdale. 480-398-4208, citizenpublichouse.com.


T. Cook's celebrates its 20th anniversary with a party on May 9 and a special menu throughout May. It's at the Royal Palms Resort and Spa, 5200 E. Camelback Road. (Photo: Courtesy of T. Cook's)

The three-course menu ($95) has seatings from 5-6:30 p.m.; and the five-course menu ($150, includes a glass of Moet &amp; Chandon) has seatings from 7:30-10 p.m. Choose dishes like lobster ravioli, winter black truffle soup, pan-roasted John Dory, American wagyu and butterscotch budino. Reservations required.


Details: , 5200 E. Camelback Road, Phoenix. 602-808-0766, royalpalmshotel.com.


Happy-hour offerings at District American Kitchen and Wine Bar include the Dirty Fries. (Photo: Courtesy of District American Kitchen and Wine Bar)

Enjoy a three-course menu ($60) from 5-10:30 p.m. Entree selections include Gorgonzola-crusted beef rib eye, black cod and green posole, chicken and biscuits and surf and turf. Start with sweet-potato bisque or a salad and finish with a black-and-white cake with raspberry dark chocolate ganache. Dinner includes a champagne toast. Reservations recommended.


Details: Sheraton Grand Phoenix, 320 N. Third St., Phoenix. 602-817-5400, sheratonphoenixdowntown.com.


The French toast and steak &amp; eggs at Gertrude's (Photo: Gertrude's)

A 32-ounce tomahawk rib eye for two ($90) and halibut cheeks ($38) are among the dinner specials served from 4-9 p.m. Also consider the Hoppin’ John with black-eyed peas, red rice, ham hock and cornbread ($12); and flourless torte with pomegranate and chocolate mousse ($10). Reservations recommended.


Details: 1201 N. Galvin Parkway, Phoenix. 480-719-8600, gertrudesrestaurant.net.


Ron Pacioni (of Coup des Tartes restaurant in Phoenix) says he started started booking Valentine's Day reservations around Christmas. (Photo: Pat Shannahan/The Arizona Republic)

A cocktail hour with champagne pours and appetizers kicks off the night at 7 p.m. A three-course meal ($99; $125 with "The Champagne Soiree Burlesque" show) features dishes like yellow fin tuna and black truffle tartar, coup escargot, veal osso bucco, Moroccan lamb shank and desserts. Reservations required.


Details: 1725 E. Osborn Road, Phoenix. 602-212-1082, nicetartes.com.


The Roasted Garlic Aioli is part of the charcuterie board from the Market by Jennifer's in Phoenix. (Photo: The Republic, Phoenix)

A three-course dinner ($75, add $20 for wine pairing) features dishes like shrimp bisque, Chateaubriand beef tenderloin, lamb chops Provence, salmon en croute and baked Alaska for dessert. Dinner includes a champagne toast. Reservations required for seatings from 5-10 p.m.


Details: 3603 E. Indian School Road, Phoenix. 602-626-5050, themarketphx.com.


Rusconi Pork: Roasted pork tenderloin at Rusconi's American Kitchen. (Photo: Joanie Simon)

Served from 5-10 p.m., a three-course dinner ($64) features options like braised beef short rib with kabocha squash puree; roasted pork tenderloin with spaghetti squash and small green beans; and open-faced Key West shrimp ravioli. End dinner with a salted caramel and Ghirardelli chocolate chip cheesecake. Reservations recommended. 


Details: 10637 N. Tatum Blvd., Phoenix. 480-483-0009, rusconiskitchen.com.


Chilean sea bass in champagne truffle sauce at Ocean Prime located at City North. (Photo: Pat Shannahan, Arizona Republic)

Specials, served from 4 p.m.-12:30 a.m., include an 8-ounce filet mignon, warm lobster and frisee salad with pancetta-shitake vinaigrette ($51); and chocolate creme brulee ($10), A champagne toast is included. Reservations recommended.


Details: 5455 E. High St., Phoenix. 480-347-1313, ocean-prime.com.


The four-course dinner and black-and-white party runs from 9:30 p.m.-1 a.m. and includes a drink, party favors, photos and champagne toast ($125). You might choose a menu that starts with one large stone crab, followed by lobster bisque, then dry-aged New York strip with loaded baked potato and lemon meringue for dessert. During the day, restaurant hours are 10 a.m.-9 p.m.   


Details: 4225 E. Camelback Road, Phoenix. 602-346-0110, buckandrider.com.


Fairmont Scottsdale Princess. (Photo: Fairmont Scottsdale Princess)

From 4-9 p.m., Toro Latin Restaurant will serve a three-course meal ($85) with choices like corn empanada, Parmesan-crusted sea scallops, braised pork shank and chipotle miso sea bass. Reservations recommended.


Details: 7575 E. Princess Drive, Scottsdale. 480-585-2694, scottsdaleprincess.com.

Alto Ristorante's four-course dinner ($90) will feature options like beef tartare, Maine lobster risotto, ricotta gnocchi and braised short rib. Southwest Bistro will feature a three-course menu ($35). Both dinners will served from 5-10 p.m. Reservations recommended.


Details: 7500 E. Doubletree Ranch Road, Scottsdale. 480-444-1234, scottsdale.hyatt.com.

Details: , 6850 E. Main St., Scottsdale. 480-376-2600, hotelvalleyho.com.


Beef Heart Sandwich at Evo. (Photo: David Wallace / The Republic)

From 4 p.m. to 2 a.m., a four-course menu ($65) kicks off with choice of starters and salad, and main dish options like lobster carbonara, eggplant ragout, veal osso buco and garganelli and duck ragu. Reservations recommended.


Details: 4175 N. Goldwater Blvd., Scottsdale. 480-265-9814, evoscottsdale.com.


Traditional eggs benedict at Lon's. (Photo: Debby Wolvos Photography)

A four-course dinner from 5-10 p.m. includes several entree choices ($105-$125), from pan-roasted duck breast to Wagyu strip loin, all served with sides. Consider smoked venison carpaccio, house-smoked pork belly and egg or seared foie gras and torchon for your other two courses. Finish with a signature dessert trio. Reservations required.


Details: The Hermosa Inn, 5532 N. Palo Cristi Road, Paradise Valley. 602-955-7878, hermosainn.com.


The baked steelhead fish at Liberty Station comes with mushrooms and berry butter. (Photo: Photos by Joanie Simon)

Celebrate with specials served from 5-10 p.m., including an 8-ounce filet mignon with Boursin cheese risotto, broccolini and chipotle demi glace ($36); and two 7-ounce pork chops with sweet potato gratin, green beans and brandy apple sauce ($30). Reservations recommended.


Details: 34522 N. Scottsdale Road, Scottsdale. 480-595-9930, libertystationtavern.com.


Air-dried duckling from Market Street Kitchen in Scottsdale. (Photo: MICHAEL CHOW, Michael Chow/The Republic)

Enjoy specials served from 5-10 p.m., including pan-seared Chilean sea bass with smashed potatoes, green beans and brown-butter lemon sauce ($42); and jumbo shrimp cocktail with Bloody Mary cocktail sauce ($18). Reservations recommended.


Details: 20825 N. Pima Road, Scottsdale. 480-278-7044, marketstreetkitchen.com.

A three-course meal ($45), served from 5-10 p.m., includes dishes like avocado and mozzarella salad; New York steak with garlic mashed potatoes and sauteed mushrooms; and chocolate and peanut butter brownie with caramel ice cream. Dinner includes a glass of sparkling wine. Reservations recommended.


Details: 20469 N. Hayden Road, Scottsdale. 480-502-6740, urbantaverngroup.com/tavernamericana.

Dine on dishes like potato leek with truffled Parmesan chip, day-boat scallops, fork-tender short rib and coconut panna cotta in a four- or six-course dinner ($65-$115). Seating times are 5:30-7:30 p.m. and 7:30 p.m. to close. Reservations recommended.


Details: 10050 N. Scottsdale Road, Scottsdale. 480-477-1390, sonatasrestaurant.com.


Niman Aged Tomahawk Rib-Eye from Hearth ’61 at Mountain Shadows in Paradise Valley. (Photo: Courtesy of the James Agency)

Linger over a champagne-paired, five-course, meal ($160 for the 6:30 p.m. seating; $190 for the 9:30 p.m. seating with a midnight champagne toast). An amuse-bouche will be followed by seared duck breast, golden king crab salad, wood-roasted Wagyu strip loin and vanilla-bean panna cotta. Reservations recommended.


Details: 5445 E. Lincoln Drive, Paradise Valley. 855-318- 8942 mountainshadows.com.

Choose dishes like white-truffle risotto, terrine of Hudson Valley foie gras, braised leeks, California guinea hen, Colorado lamb rack and Barolo-braised beef cheeks. Reservations required.


Details: 5700 E. McDonald Drive, Paradise Valley. 480-607-2300, sanctuaryaz.com.

The former features choices like smoked salmon bellini with caviar; veal cheek panzerotti with parsnip puree; and king crab with Calabrian chile cavatelli. The latter includes options like roasted pork tenderloin, lobster potpie and beef Wellington. Reservations recommended.

The Horny Toad in Cave Creek. (Photo: Horny Toad)

From 4-10 p.m., dinner ($25) will offer choices like an 8-ounce prime rib, half fried chicken or broiled cod, all accompanied by sides, dessert and a champagne toast. Reservations recommended.


Details: 6738 E. Cave Creek Road, Cave Creek. 480-488-9542, thehornytoad.com.

At Ko’Sin, a four-course dinner ($80, $72 for ages 55 and older) will be served from 5:30-9 p.m.  Consider the “Moon and Sea” with grilled lobster, blackened corvina, lemon-mezzelune pasta, chanterelle mushroom and champagne cream; or the “South for the Winter” with duck breast, black garlic gnocchi, fig reduction, cauliflower espuma and duck cracklings. Kai will serve a formal seven-course meal from 5-9 p.m. ($220, add $100 for wine pairing). Reservations recommended.


Details: 5594 Wild Horse Pass Blvd., Gila River Indian Reservation. 602-385-5726, wildhorsepassresort.com.  


Cap’n Crunch Crusted French Toast prepared at the Culinary Dropout. (Photo: Fox Restaurant Concepts)

A three-course menu ($25) starts with smoked salmon toast or sweet-potato pierogies with vegetables. Other choices include fried chicken, rainbow trout, apple cobbler and chocolate s’mores pudding. The menu will be served 4 p.m.-midnight. Fox Restaurant Concepts restaurants offering prix-fixe menus include North Italia ($55) and Little Cleo’s Seafood Legend at the Yard ($30). Reservations recommended.


Details: 149 S. Farmer Ave., Tempe. 480-240-1601. Other locations at culinarydropout.com, foxrc.com.


Celebrate New Year’s Eve with House of Tricks (Photo: House of Tricks)

From from 5-10 p.m., the restaurant will serve specials like rabbit and spinach chorizo terrine ($15); bison meatloaf with purple sweet potato puree ($26); blackened ahi tuna with honey-wasabi drizzle ($33); and salted caramel-apple bread pudding ($9). Reservations recommended.


Details: 114 E. Seventh St., Tempe. 480 968-1114, houseoftricks.com. 


Zappone's Italian Bistro in Gilbert on Tuesday, July 29, 2014. (Photo: David Wallace, David Wallace/The Republic)

A three-course dinner ($35-$40, add $20 for wine pairing) will be served from 4-10 p.m. Start with wild mushroom soup or an arugula salad with candied walnuts, Gorgonzola and apples. Entree choices are filet mignon with truffle whipped potatoes and roasted baby carrots; or crab ravioli with vodka sauce. Dessert is mascarpone cheesecake. Reservations required.


Details: 1652 N. Higley Road, Suite 103, Gilbert. 480-218-2338, zapponesbistro.com.


Shula's Steak House will be serving guests at the Wild Horse Pass Hotel &amp; Casino. (Photo: Nick Oza/The Republic)

Shula’s Steak House will serve a four-course dinner ($95) from 11 a.m.-10 p.m., featuring options like shrimp cocktail, house salad, filet mignon, seared scallops and cheesecake for dessert. Café 24/7 will serve an Italian surf and turf dinner ($16) from 11 a.m.-11 p.m. Walk-ins OK.


Details: 5040 Wild Horse Pass Blvd., Gila River Reservation. 800-946-4452, wingilariver.com/wild-horse-pass.


Amuse Bouche Bistro (Photo: Deirdre Hamill/The Republic)

This BYOB will serve an amuse of mushroom vol au vent before a three-course meal ($75, includes glass charge). Choose a crispy pork belly or lobster roll starter, followed by seared diver scallops or hickory-smoked beef tenderloin. Dessert is poached pear and almond cream tarte with pistachio panna cotta. Reservations recommended for 4:30-8:30 p.m. seatings.

Serra Gaucha Brazilian Steakhouse. (Photo: Courtesy of Serra Gaucha Brazilian Steakhouse)

This new Brazilian steakhouse offers a traditional “churrasco” experience with unlimited meat cuts served tableside and a salad bar ($44.95). Open from 11:30 a.m.-9 p.m.

Litchfield's at the Wigwam Resort in Litchfield Park on March 15, 2016. (Photo: David Wallace/The Republic)

A four-course menu ($69) served from 5-10 p.m. features options like lobster bisque, branzino with black lentils, prime New York steak with lump crab and sides, diver sea scallops and dessert. Reservations required.

Stop by IronWorks Restaurant for dancing and great food. (Photo: Ironworks Restaurant)

Choose a salad or crab bisque to start, and finish with raspberry cheesecake or creme brulee. Reservations recommended.
